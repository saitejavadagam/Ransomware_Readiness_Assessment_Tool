Initial Compromise

The first thing an attacker must do is gain entry into a system, often by tricking a user into downloading an infected file or clicking a malicious link. Your first line of defense is to block these actions.

Lateral Movement

Once an attacker is in your system, they attempt to move around the network to cause damage or steal valuable assets. Many legacy security tools allow broad access to valuable assets once an attacker gets in.

Data Loss/Exfiltration

Many attacks include data theft, such as PII, financial data, or intellectual property. Your security controls must recognize and stop attempts to steal valuable data.
